Tribute to the Missile Man of India – Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Indian scientist and statesman

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, known as the "Missile Man of India," was an influential aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007.

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was one of India’s most respected scientists and the 11th President of India. Known as the Missile Man of India, he played a vital role in developing India’s missile and nuclear programs and dedicated his life to education, innovation, and national development.

Born in a humble family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Dr. Kalam rose through hardship and became a leading aerospace engineer at ISRO and DRDO. He was instrumental in the development of the SLV-III, Agni, and Prithvi missiles, and contributed to India’s 1998 Pokhran-II nuclear tests.

He served as the President of India from 2002 to 2007, earning the title “People’s President” due to his simplicity and connection with youth. After his presidency, he continued to engage with students and wrote inspirational books like Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, and India 2020.

Dr. Kalam believed deeply in the power of dreams, education, and youth. He inspired millions with quotes like:

“Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action.”
“If you want to shine like a sun, first burn like a sun.”
-A.P.J Abdul Kalam
Career and Achievements

     Kalam began his career at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO), where he worked on hovercraft design. In 1969, he joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he became the project director of the SLV-III, India's first satellite launch vehicle, which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980. His contributions to missile technology led to the development of key missiles such as Agni and Prithvi, earning him the title "Missile Man of India".
      In 1998, Kalam played a pivotal role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests, which established India as a nuclear power. His vision for India's technological advancement culminated in the Technology Vision 2020 project, aimed at transforming India into a developed nation



Contributions to Science

      Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam made remarkable contributions to science and technology in India, especially in the fields of aerospace engineering, missile development, and space research. He played a leading role in the development of India’s first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully placed the Rohini satellite into orbit in 1980, marking India’s entry into the space age.
    Kalam also contributed significantly to India’s nuclear capabilities, playing a key role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998. Apart from defense and space technologies, he also promoted the use of science for societal development by supporting projects in healthcare, agriculture, and rural development. Through his vision document "Technology Vision 2020," he aimed to transform India into a developed nation using science and innovation. His efforts not only strengthened India's scientific and defense capabilities but also inspired millions of students to pursue careers in science and technology.

      He passed away on 27 July 2015 while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong — living his passion till his last breath.

     Dr. Kalam was honored with India’s highest civilian award, the Bharat Ratna, and left behind a legacy of vision, humility, and inspiration.
